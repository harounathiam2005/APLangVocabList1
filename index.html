Ministerial

    Meaning: Playing an important role in causing an action or event.
  • Synonyms: Instrumental, Involved


    • A button is often depicted as a cause of an action. It is ministerial because it plays a key role in a thing.

  • The death of Archduke Franz-Ferdinand was ministerial in the declaration of war that began WWI.

    • Indictment

      Meaning: A thing that indicates that a system is not right; wrong.
  • Synonym: Imputation
  • Antonym: Aquittal


    • An indictment, which could be a formal charge ussued, is also an indication of a thing being not good. It's similar to a judgemt being made about the state of a system; hence the judge.

  • This trash on the side of the road is an indictment of the waning sense of cleanliness in our area.

    • Sardonic

      Meaning: Humorous or witty; often in a malicious manner.
  • Synonym: Petty
  • Antonym: Respectful


    • Frieza from the Dragon Ball series is often shown to embody sardonicism. His actions and speech as an antagonist are mocking to the heroes and very cynical.

  • His demeanor was sardonic, an indicator of unapologeticness for what he has done.

    • Altruistic

      Meaning: Emphasizing the importance and well-being of others; acting selflessly.
  • Synonyms: Selfless, Self-sacraficing


    • Altruisticness is a key part of heroship. Therefore, the image that depicts the word "hero" also includes selflessness.

  • He acted altruistically, saving the child from the fire.

    • Ingratiate

      Meaning: To gain praise through purposeful effort.
  • Synonym: Flatter
  • Antonym: Condemn


    • Ingratiation requires effort to please a party that results in that party showing respect. In Naruto, he works hard to get the people to acknowledge him and respect him.

  • After working through medical school for years, he ingratiated his father so that he would finally respect him.

      Meaning: Substitution of a word with a very close associasion of that word.
  • Synonyms: Substitution, Word-replacement


    • This picture shows a buisiness man, which are often referred to as "suits". This is a case of metonymy.

  • *Referring to a group of large gymgoers* "Take a look at those muscles over there."

    • Antecedent

      Meaning: Something that came before another.
  • Synonym: Ancestor
  • Antonym: Descendent


    • The iPhone 3G precedes the modern Apple lineup. It is an antecedent of the iPhone X, 11, 12, and so on.

  • The antecedent of the human race is thought to be primates.

    • Syllogism

      Meaning: A form of reasoning in which a conclusion is made through acceptance of two given arguments.
  • Synonym: Deduction
  • Antonym: Induction


    • The image depicts a police investigator, whose job often involves reasoning to determing a conclusion. Syllogism or other deductive skills might be used.

  • Computers are non-living things; non-living things are stupid; therefore, computers must be stupid.

    • Mythos

  • Synonyms: Stories, Legend


    • The minoutaur is a well-known symbol of Greek mythology. It represent mythos in this sense.

  • The mythos of the Hydra is one of grotesque detail.

    Pompous

      Meaning: Irritatingly grand or self-important.
  • Synonym: Extravagant
  • Antonym: Humble


    • Gideon from Gravity Falls is known to be a bragadocious "better than you" type character. He embodies annoying gandeur, or pomposity.

  • His pompous presentation and demeanor caused the team to not like him straight away.

    • Pleonasm

      Meaning: The use of more words than necessary to convey meaning.
  • Antonyms: Concise, To-the-point


    • Young children are great, but sometimes they may talk to much or give too much information. You could say that they are pleonastic.

  • The students wished for him to silence himself; but the old teacher was too far ingulfed in his pleonastic tale.

    • Embellish

      Meaning: To make something more pleasant or attractive.
  • Synonyms: Decorate, Beautify


    • Glitter is in many cases used to embellish posters, projects, or even faces. Glitter is a great example of beautifying to an extended degree.

  • She thought that adding lots of sprinkles would embellish the cake; but it really just made it harder to see the beautiful frosting.

    • Polemic

      Meaning: Heavy critisim/attack of something.
  • Antonyms: Light commentary, discourse


    • An example of a polemic would be Martin Luther's 99 Theses. They were considered attacks against the catholic church.

  • "His shameless polemics against the crown will not go unpunished."

    • Reverantly

      Meaning: With an extended degree of respect.
  • Synonym: Solemn
  • Antonym: Cynical


    • The image depicts prayer, in which it is infinitely emphasized to maintain respect and decency. A reverant attitude is sought when performing such an action.

  • He reverantly bowed his head in front of the king's chariot when it arrived.
